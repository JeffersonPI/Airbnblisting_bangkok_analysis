# Airbnb Bangkok: From Underperforming Listings to High Demand

## Overview
This project analyzes Airbnb listings in Bangkok to understand why many listings underperform despite high availability, and to identify the most effective strategies to improve demand.

The analysis focuses on the relationship between supply, demand, and listing performance using data-driven insights.

---

## Objectives
- Identify key factors affecting Airbnb listing performance  
- Analyze the relationship between price, availability, and demand  
- Determine the most impactful strategies to increase demand  

---

## Dataset
- Source: Airbnb Listings Dataset  
- Total listings: 15,854  
- Key variables:
  - `price` → Listing price  
  - `number_of_reviews` → Demand (proxy)  
  - `availability_365` → Supply  
  - `room_type` → Listing category  

> Note: The dataset does not include transaction data (bookings/revenue), so demand is estimated using number of reviews.

---

## Methodology
- Data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Correlation and pattern analysis  
- Simulation-based strategy evaluation  

---

## Key Findings
- Many listings have high availability but low demand → underutilization  
- Weak relationship between price and demand  
- Underperformance is widespread across listings  

Listing performance is driven more by **competitiveness** than pricing.

---

## Core Insight
The main issue is not pricing, but weak listing competitiveness.

Demand is primarily influenced by:
- **Quality (attractiveness)** → photos, descriptions, amenities  
- **Visibility (discoverability)** → search ranking, exposure  

---

## Strategy Impact (Simulation-Based)
- Quality improvement → **~30% increase in demand**  
- Visibility improvement → **~20% increase in demand**  
- Pricing adjustment → **~15% increase in demand**  

---

## Business Impact
Improving listing quality can increase demand from:
- ~16 reviews → ~21 reviews (**+30%**)

This indicates significant potential for increasing bookings and revenue.

---

## Conclusion
The market is not constrained by supply, but by underperforming listings.

Improving listing competitiveness is the most effective way to increase demand.

---

## Recommendations
- Prioritize **quality improvements** (highest impact)  
- Improve **visibility** through optimization and exposure  
- Use **pricing as a supporting strategy**, not the main driver  

---

## Limitations
Results are based on simulation assumptions and should be validated with real-world data.
